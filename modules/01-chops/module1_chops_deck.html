Where we start

The model most of us start with

Most of us met CHOPs as "the audio and number nodes" and wired them by feel — LFO into a parameter, a Math to scale it, a Lag to smooth it. That intuition ships real shows. It carries you a long way.

It just doesn't tell you when CHOPs are the right vehicle and when they'll sink your frame rate. That part usually gets passed around as folklore. Here's the picture underneath it instead.

The core · slot 2

The train runs when
it's called — not on a timetable

A train sits in the yard until the destination down the line dispatches for it: "send me the current cargo." The order travels up the route; the train runs down it. A spur nobody dispatched to stays cold — parked cars, costing nothing.

re-haulDispatched AND something upstream changed → the route actually re-runs, cars re-weighed.
waved throughDispatched but cargo unchanged → the city waves through what's already staged on the platform. No work.
The core · slot 2

What a run costs

Every operation happens per car, every time the train runs. So the whole bill is three numbers multiplied together:

cars×cities×how often
free4 cars · 10 cities · dispatched once when you turn a knob.
fine4 cars · 10 cities · 60×/sec because one city is live. Still nothing.
bites20,000 cars · 10 cities · 60×/sec → the yard never stops.
Don't overcook. Make the PC's life easier.
The core · slot 2

One track. One train
at a time.

There is a single track. One train runs it at a time, car by car, in order — no parallel lines. That's TD's single-threaded CPU world.

Rail is wonderful for a sensible consist on a clear track. It does not become an airline by adding cars. When the cargo gets huge, the answer is never "a longer train."

Right way vs the trap · slot 3

Keep CHOPs doing
CHOP-shaped work

rightPer-fixture, per-parameter, per-control values — dozens to a few hundred cars. Master dimmer, rig intensities, smoothing, audio bands → parameters. Rail is built for exactly this.
trapReaching for CHOPs because they're familiar, then pushing per-pixel / per-point data through them — thousands of cars, re-hauled every frame, on one track.
When a chain gets heavy, don't count nodes — count cars.
Conceptual precision

Timeline vs time-slice

Timeline channel: every car of the journey is laid along the track ahead. The whole trip exists at once — stand anywhere on the route and look at that car. (Scrub to any frame.)

Time-sliced channel: no track laid ahead. Cars exist only as they cross the frame-wide platform, in blocks, then they're gone unless an operation stored them.

A time-slice is not one sample. It's every sample that fell within this frame's window.
The worked case · audio

Audio: two clocks,
not one

Three numbers all sound like "the audio" and they measure different things. Name them apart and the fog clears.

cargoThe audio line delivers 48000 cars per second — its sample rate. Nothing to do with TD.
pickupTD opens the platform 60 times per second — its frame rate. Nothing to do with the audio.

Time-slicing is just the gearbox between the two clocks.

The worked case · audio

Do the arithmetic
out loud

48000 cars/sec ÷ 60 frames/sec ≈ 800 cars/frame
~800 raw samples land in every frame's slice

That's the whole mystery. Drop to 30 fps and the platform opens half as often → ~1600 cars per slice. Cars-per-second is fixed by the source; cars-per-frame is just that divided by how often the window opened.

The worked case · audio

FFT is a different
kind of operation

Every city so far reweighed cars in place: 800 in, 800 out, same count, new weights — working along the train, in the time domain (a car = loudness at a successive instant).

An FFT (Audio Spectrum CHOP) melts the whole block down and recasts it as a different train — cars are now frequency bands. Ask for 256 bands → 256 cars out, not 800. The car count changed and what a car means changed.

Time domain: cars are moments. Frequency domain: cars are pitch buckets. FFT transforms one into the other.
Why it's a cost decision

FFT is also a
reduction

You do not want to haul ~800 raw audio cars per frame into your control logic — that's airline cargo back on the rails.

→
800 time-domain cars → the 8 bands you actually use
Get the audio, reduce it to the few bands you need immediately, then route those.
